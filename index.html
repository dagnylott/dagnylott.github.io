Matlab

(Spring 2020)

At the start of the pandemic, the Washington Post released a model that measured the effect of social distancing on the spread of COVID-19. Although their model did not have any scientific importance, I thought it did a great job of conveying how social distancing can flatten the curve, so I tried to mimick it in one of my classes using Matlab. To do this I used modeling particle dynamics, graphics, real-time animation, user interaction, data analysis, and other features like buttons and callback functions.

Unlike their model, mine allows for the user to adjust two parameters, giving the user a lot more information and the ability to recreate a new scenario each time. For context, a Gallup poll at the end of April reported that approximately 80% of the U.S. population was following the social distancing guidlines.

SolidWorks

(Spring 2020)

On the left is a SolidWorks animation of a soap dispenser CAD model that I designed for my Mechanical Design class. I took apart the dispenser, recorded basic measurements to ensure the proportions were right, and incorporated the motion of the spring into my model.

Note: Since taking this class, I have gained a lot more CAD experience, mostly through internships as well as in class




Laser Cutting

(Spring 2020)

I designed this rocking horse when I first started using SolidWorks and was happily surprised by how well it turned out. I used a laser cutter to cut clear acrylic for the horse's body and MDF for the base. The horse has three types of joints: press fittings, single-lap joints, and two t-nut/tab & slot connections.




Water Show

(Summers 2017 & 2018)

For two summers I directed my local pool's Water Show – an annual theatrical play in and around the pool. Yes that means wet costumes, choreographed scenes with kickboards and noodles, and of course splashing! All-in-all, it required a great deal of preparation but it was a good learning experience and a lot of fun. The first show was Robin Hood followed by Mulan (pictured below).

Some of the cast wait behind the front office for the show to commence. Meanwhile the Huns, in gray shirts and warrior paint, prepare their sword-fighting skills for battle. Soon after, their face paint shortly washes off in the pool.


Fireworks explode to celebrate Mulan’s victory and a job well done. The cast members line up behind the diving board before jumping into the pool one-by-one and finishing the last choreographed routine.
